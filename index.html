Tracing The Arctic Regions: Mapping William Bradford's 1869 Photography Expedition to Greenland

In the summer of 1869, the American marine painter William Bradford began an unprecedented journey to the Arctic. While others had ventured into the region before him, Bradford’s expedition from Boston, Massachusetts to the western coast of Greenland was the first of its kind planned solely for the purposes of art.

This expedition was not done in the service of any queen our country, nor for scientific study. Instead, this perilous trip to the Arctic was “solely for the purposes of art,” as Bradford wrote in the book that documented the journey, titled The Arctic Regions: Illustrated with Photographs Taken on an Art Expedition to Greenland.

“Tracing The Arctic Regions” is a spatial art history project that explores this book, its photographs, and its geographic meanings. Developed at the Center for Spatial and Textual Analysis (CESTA) and the Department of Art & Art History at Stanford University.
